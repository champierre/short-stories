# The Lavender Train

Ruu's favorite thing in the whole world is the new lavender-colored train on the Inokashira Line.

Every morning, Ruu sits by the window and watches the trains.
All kinds of trains rumble past the railroad crossing near his house, clackety-clack.

A bluish train.
A white train.
But the one Ruu loves most of all is the lavender-colored train.

"I wonder if it'll come today."

Ruu said in a tiny voice.
Then his mother smiled and said:

"I hope so."

Whenever Ruu sees the lavender train, his chest feels warm and glowing.
Its gentle color makes his heart feel bright, even on cloudy days.

That morning, before going to kindergarten, Ruu went to the railroad crossing.
Clang, clang, clang — the signal sounded and the crossing gate slowly came down.

"Is this the one? Is this the one?"

Ruu's heart was pounding.

First, a white train glided past.
Then, a bluish train went by.
Ruu felt a little disappointed.

"Maybe it's not coming today…"

And then.

Far down the tracks, a soft, gentle color appeared.
Catching the light, the lavender shimmered faintly.

"There it is!"

Ruu jumped up with joy.

The lavender train rumbled past — clackety-clack, clackety-clack — right in front of him.
Its windows sparkled, and the body of the train was a gentle color, just like a flower.

Ruu waved his little hands back and forth.

"Bye-byeee!"

A young woman sitting by the window smiled and waved back at him.

Ruu was so happy that his cheeks turned round.

When he arrived at kindergarten, Ruu told his teacher right away:

"I saw the lavender train today!"

The teacher's eyes went wide.

"Really? How wonderful! What color was it like?"

Ruu spread his arms wide and said:

"A gentle color! Soft and fluffy and beautiful!"

His friends gathered around him too.

"I wanna see it!"
"Me too!"
"Where does it run?"

Ruu felt so very happy.
When he talked about the things he loved, everyone got excited too.
That made Ruu happiest of all.

During drawing time, Ruu drew the train.
Long windows.
A shiny body.
And a gentle lavender color.

"Done!"

In Ruu's picture, there was a big train, and standing in front of it, waving with a big smile, was Ruu himself.

When his mother came to pick him up, she looked at the drawing and said:

"That's lovely. You really do love that train, don't you, Ruu?"

Ruu nodded with a big, big nod.

On the way home, as they passed by the crossing, the lavender train came along once more.

In the glow of the sunset, its color looked even gentler than in the morning.

Ruu waved and thought to himself:

I hope I get to see you again tomorrow.

The lavender train rumbled softly through the town, clackety-clack, and disappeared into the distance.
